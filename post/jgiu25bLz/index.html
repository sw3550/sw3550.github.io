EME6414 Week 10 #1 Divided World in the Era of Web 2.0

07/19/2023

I didn't experience the golden age of the Internet, a time when the barrier between the Chinese and English online worlds wasn't so apparent. However, yesterday, I stumbled upon something that made me acutely aware of the vast gap that still exists between these two realms.

I came across a Chinese caricature that portrayed the pandemic as a thief, stealing three precious years from everyone's lives. Intrigued by its poignant message, I wanted to share it with my English-speaking friends. But when I typed in my search query on Google, the world's most popular search engine, I was met with disappointing results. The caricature seemed to be nowhere to be found.

Refusing to give up, I then turned to Baidu, the prominent Chinese search engine. I thought that since the caricature was originally in Chinese, I might have better luck there. However, even on Baidu, my efforts to locate the artwork proved futile. Instead, I found an abundance of caricatures that aimed to convey positive energy during the challenging times of the pandemic. While these illustrations were undoubtedly uplifting, they weren't what I was looking for.

In that moment, the realization hit me like a ton of bricks. Despite the vastness of the Internet and the incredible potential for global connection, there still existed a profound disconnect between the Chinese and English online worlds. The caricature I sought probably existed somewhere on the Chinese web, yet it remained hidden from my view, lost in the vast sea of content that I couldn't access due to language barriers.

It was disheartening to think that despite all the progress we've made in the digital age, there were still parts of the online world that remained isolated, separated by linguistic differences. The Internet, with its boundless information and instant connectivity, was supposed to unite us, breaking down barriers and fostering cross-cultural understanding. But in this moment, I couldn't shake the feeling of lossâ€”the loss of an opportunity to connect with the thoughts, emotions, and experiences of people in a different part of the world.

This experience also served as a reminder of the importance of linguistic diversity and inclusivity on the Internet. As we move forward, striving to make the Internet a more interconnected and accessible place for everyone, we must work towards bridging these gaps. It's not just about making information available in different languages but also about enabling meaningful interactions and fostering mutual understanding between people of diverse linguistic and cultural backgrounds.
