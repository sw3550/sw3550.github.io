Literature | Direct Instruction Revisited: A Key Model for Instructional Tech @Mahiaro, Lockee, & Burton

05/02/2022 2 min read

Purpose
Present the DI model with the notion that the designer can and should use the model effectively based on an appropriate assessment of the learners, content, context, and task at hand.
Behavioral theory
Key components: modeling, reinforcement, feedback, and successive approximations. - Modeling with reinforced guided performance.
Preassumption
Physical characteristics evolve or are selected by the environment. (Behaviorism)
Those working behaviors are selected by the consequences that follow the behavior.
Learners must be active (behaving) to learn.
Learning is universal, in the sense that selectionist principles are involved in learning planaria to people...
Behaviorally based models reject logical positivism, mentalists such as mind, and free will. - passionate reactions.

Shared key components
Materials and curriculum are broken down into small steps
The objective must be stated clearly and in terms of learner outcomes or performance
Learners are provided with opportunities to connect their new knowledge with what they already know
Learners are given practice with each step or combination of steps
Learners experience additional opportunities to practice that promotes increasing responsibility and independence
Feedback is provided after each practice opportunity or set of practice opportunities.

  • Three-Steps
    Introduction
    The main presentation of the lesson
    Practice

Teaching models
Engelmann's DI model
Rosenshine's Explicit Teaching model
Good & Grow's Strategies for effective Teaching model
Hunter's Design of Effective lesson model
Gagne's events of instruction model

Introduction
Gaining attention - auditory and visual stimuli
informing the learner of the objective - appropriate media types
Main presentation
Stimulating recall of prerequisite learning - quizzing or assessment tools
presenting the stimulus materials - appropriate media types
providing learner guidance
Practice
Eliciting the performance - teacher-led through telecommunication or self-paced through computer-based interactions.
Providing feedback with performance correctness, assessing the performance, enhancing retention and transfer.
